Event Report

AKTU Incubation Visit.

National PG College E-Cell organized a guided industrial visit to the Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU) Incubation Centre on April 20, 2026. The experience connected UDYAM students with incubation best practices, government funding pathways, and live innovation labs.

Visit Overview

The UDYAM Entrepreneurship Cell (E-Cell) of National Post Graduate College (NPGC) recently organized an insightful industrial visit to the Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU) Incubation Centre. Supported by the Samadhan Group under the Hunar Career Growth Program, the visit offered students a firsthand look into the thriving startup ecosystem, incubation processes, and cutting-edge technological innovations.

The trip was supported by Samadhan Group's Hunar Career Growth Program and led by NPGC faculty and mentors who helped students uncover how startup ecosystems are built around mentoring, funding, and technical infrastructure.

Deconstructing the Startup Ecosystem

The visit began at 12:00 PM in the E-Cell Meeting Room of the AKTU Innovation Hub. Dignitaries introduced the fundamentals of entrepreneurship and outlined government policies designed to scale grassroots ideas.

  • Ideation Stage: A grant of up to ₹1 Lakh to develop concepts, including 3 to 12 months of dedicated cabin space, mentorship, and free lab access.
  • Scalability Stage: A monthly sustenance allowance of ₹17,500 for up to one year.
  • Sustainability Stage: A growth grant of ₹17.5 Lakhs with a minimum 26% founder shareholding.
  • Prototype Stage: A dedicated development allowance of ₹5 Lakhs.
  • Intellectual Property Support: Free patenting assistance for incubated startups through the Kalam Patent Centre.

Inside the Innovation Hub

The tour covered three core areas of the AKTU Incubation Centre, giving students a strong understanding of both the hardware and research support that powers startup growth.

1. The Electronics Section & Idea Lab

Students explored the co-working spaces and the Idea Lab corridor. In the Electronics Section, they examined specialized equipment like 3D printers, vinyl cutters, and advanced soldering stations. Notable innovations on display included:

  • A cash-disinfecting machine widely adopted by the State Bank of India (SBI) during the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • A specialized medical assistance robot used in healthcare facilities.

2. The Mechanical & Heavy Machinery Sections

The mechanical workshop showcased rapid prototyping tools such as hot air guns, corded hand grinders, nail guns, and hand rivet guns. Students also observed heavy industrial automation equipment:

  • Pallet conveyor systems
  • SCARA (Selective Compliance Assembly Robot Arm) robots
  • Automatic grinding machinery
  • Single-handling industrial robots

3. The Artificial Intelligence Lab

The final stop was the AI Lab, featuring a session led by an NPGC alumnus currently working there as an AI Scientist. Students received a practical breakdown of core AI concepts:

  • Dataset management and processing
  • Supervised vs. unsupervised learning
  • Reinforcement learning using labelled and unlabelled data

Shaping Future Entrepreneurs

The industrial visit concluded with a certificate distribution ceremony hosted by the accompanying NPGC faculty members. By bridging the gap between classroom theory and real-world industrial infrastructure, this visit provided UDYAM E-Cell members with invaluable networking opportunities, practical insights into the startup lifecycle, and the inspiration needed to turn their innovative ideas into reality.

Visit Highlights

  • Date April 20, 2026
  • Delegation Size 26 students from various academic departments
  • Mentors & Guides Mentored by Abhishek Sir and Devansh Sir (Samadhan Group). Led by NPGC faculty members Jyoti Bhargava Ma’am, Dr. Shubham Mishra Sir, Neeraj Sir, and Osceen Ma’am.
  • Core Objective To educate budding entrepreneurs about government funding, machinery access, technical guidance, and institutional support provided by AKTU.
